Meet the Wndr(er)

A film director doesn’t just look at the world.
They notice it.

The sway of trees in the wind. The reflection of clouds on water. The shadow stretching across a doorway. The texture of a weathered wall. The ripple of movement in a busy street. The way sound and silence shape a moment.

What’s the story here? How does this moment feel? Who are we meant to be watching, and what happens next?

They see life not as a blur, but as a series of shots and scenes, each one carefully framed by perspective and emotion. To them, every ordinary moment holds structure: a beginning, a middle, an end, and a feeling beating quietly at its heart.

Wndr(er).

The Wndr(er) is a filmmaker before they know the word.

They pause where others move on,
framing the ordinary
until it becomes extraordinary.

How it works

Panels become shots.
Page turns become edits.
Flipbooks become animation.

a flipbook, alive ↑
A panel is a rectangle drawn around a moment. It says: look at this one thing, now.
A gutter is the gap between panels. Where the viewer does the work, filling the blank, making the leap.
A flipbook is animation at its most basic. Draw, flick through, the dot walks. The whole medium in a gesture.

Why Scotland. Why now.

Scotland has built something rare. A national screen curriculum that takes children’s filmmaking seriously from the earliest years.

WndrKlub is designed to live inside that ambition. It teaches sequencing, visual storytelling and media literacy through hands-on making. Exactly what Screen Scotland’s Film and Screen Education curriculum asks for. It supports intentional play, child-led creativity and responsive adult facilitation. Exactly what Realising the Ambition calls for.

It gives teachers a tool that works without specialist training, without equipment, without a screen in the room. A five-minute episode, a folded piece of paper, and a pencil. That is the whole kit.

Most children watch. Few of them make.
WndrKlub changes that ratio.

Film & Screen Education Screen Scotland. Sequencing, visual storytelling, media literacy. Taught panel by panel.

Curriculum for Excellence, Early Level Confidence, collaboration, emotional expression. One frame at a time.
